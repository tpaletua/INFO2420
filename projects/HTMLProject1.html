Website Critiques

    Questions:

    1. What was your first impression of this site?
    2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
    3. Does the site structure make sense, or does it confuse you? Why?
    4. Does the site's graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
    5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
    6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
    7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
    8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
    9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
    10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

    Website Critque - Trader Joes

    www.traderjoes.com

    1. My first impression of the site is that it's very clean and aesthetically pleasing to the eye. I love the layout of this website. The color scheme and pictures used seem very cohesive and intentional.
    2. They have a three-line tab that has a dropdown list of all the information categorized into sections like products, discover, recipes, list, careers, etc.
    3. The site structure to me makes sense. It's how many of the websites that I visit are structured, so it was pretty easy to navigate.
    4. The graphics and multimedia definitely enhance the site. I shop a lot at Trader Joe's, and this website encompasses their brand really well. The packaging and brand are reflected very well on the site.
    5. The load time was pretty fast.
    6. The text color and size were very readable to me. They did a great job at balancing when and where to use a bigger font size and different font styles. They did have an image as the background right as you got into the website, but I think it was nice that they added something plain on top of it to break up the space.
    7. They did have a navigation tool on the top left side of the website. It is very well organized and very easy to find what you are looking for.
    8. The site had a very cohesive color scheme. It was repeated on all pages, but every page was different, yet still tied back to the original home page theme.
    9. The site responds very well to small window sizes. Adapted well to the variations in size.
    10. On the homepage, as you scroll down, there are different sections like the "What's New?" section and the "Believe-Eat-Or-Not-Recipes" section. These sections allow you are able to swipe through the different slides they have and still stay on the home page without it taking you to a new page. It makes it fun and interactive. It's nice that it is short and easy to read.
